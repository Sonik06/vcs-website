History and Modern Trends in Version Control Systems

Version control systems (VCS) are crucial tools for tracking changes to files over time. They enable team collaboration, maintain a detailed history of edits, and make it easy to revert to previous versions when necessary. ere you can explore the evolution of version control, from early centralized models to today's distributed systems. his guide compares popular tools such as CVS, Subversion, and Git, and explains the differences between centralized and distributed approaches. You'll also learn about modern trends like GitOps, integration with CI/CD pipelines, cloud-based platforms, and the role of AI in version control. Real-world examples and case studies are included to demonstrate how these systems are used in practice.
